Urban Resource Distribution Dashboards


Project Summary

As the team lead of a five-member group, I spearheaded a data-driven urban equity project to uncover spatial disparities in public resource allocation and investment across Cary, NC. We processed and visualized over 100,000 records from nine municipal datasets, aiming to identify underdeveloped ZIP-code areas and provide data-driven support for more equitable urban planning decisions.


What We Did

  • Utilized SQL and Python to clean, integrate, and analyze a wide range of municipal data, including demographics, public infrastructure, safety incidents, and construction activity.
  • Built interactive Tableau dashboards combining geospatial maps, time-series line charts, word clouds, and KPI indicators to provide a multidimensional view of public resource allocation, enabling stakeholders to visually compare public facility investment, service accessibility, and construction trends.
  • Translated visual insights into policy recommendations, highlighting geographic imbalances and proposing targeted strategies to redirect resources, prioritize investments, and ensure sustainable urban growth.

Our dashboards support strategic urban planning decisions such as:

  • Emergency response planning and fire/police resource allocation
  • Commercial development planning based on localized brand presence
  • Forecasting infrastructure pressure in fast-growing neighborhoods
  • Attracting investment to underserved areas through targeted incentives

After years of studying mathematics and building a strong theoretical foundation, I wanted to see how those ideas could be turned into real-world impact. I joined Duke's MQM program to bridge that gap — to take the technical foundation I had built and apply it to practical problems that matter in business and society.


What I Gained

My time at Fuqua was as intense as it was rewarding. In the classroom, I gained cutting-edge knowledge and practical skills that helped me grow into a data professional. I earned a 3.98 GPA and was honored as a Fuqua Scholar — a recognition awarded to the top 10% of the class. And I had the chance to work on real-world datasets and collaborate with companies, learning directly from fantastic professors who brought both academic depth and business experience.

One of the most unforgettable moments was our capstone project. The data we collected, the model we built, and the presentation we delivered truly helped our client solve a tough business challenge and shape their strategy. When they told us, "It really helped us a lot." — that simple sentence meant more than any grade.


How I Changed

Before Fuqua, I was someone who preferred to work independently — focused, efficient, and self-reliant. But at Fuqua, everything was built around intense team collaboration. Whether it was late-night project crunches or navigating diverse viewpoints, I learned how to lead with empathy, communicate under pressure, and genuinely enjoy building something together with others. Along the way, I built friendships that I know will last a lifetime — people I laughed with, struggled with, and succeeded with.
